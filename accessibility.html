Accessibility Statement

Effective date: 21 June 2026 · Applies to thaitripplanner.com

Genext Information Systems is committed to making thaitripplanner.com usable by as many people as possible, including people who rely on assistive technology. Accessibility is an ongoing effort, and we welcome feedback that helps us improve.

1. Conformance target

We aim to align with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA. While we cannot guarantee full conformance at all times, these guidelines shape how we build and maintain the Service. This statement reflects our current status and is reviewed periodically.

2. Measures we take

  • Responsive, mobile-first layout that adapts from small phones to large screens and supports browser zoom and text resizing.
  • Semantic HTML with labelled form controls and a skip-to-content link, so screen readers can announce each field and jump past navigation.
  • Keyboard operability for the step-by-step planner, buttons, sliders and links, with a visible focus indicator.
  • Light and dark themes with color choices tested for contrast; key figures and itinerary details are presented as text, not images.
  • Reduced-motion support — decorative animations are disabled automatically when your system requests reduced motion.
  • No flashing or strobing content that could trigger photosensitive reactions.
  • Plain-language guidance at each step of the planner, one question per screen.
  • Alternative text on photographs and meaningful graphics; the route map is accompanied by a text itinerary that carries the same information.

3. Known limitations

We are transparent about areas we are still improving:

  • Range sliders (trip length, preference scales) are easiest with a pointer; we continue to refine keyboard fine-control and provide on-screen values alongside them.
  • The drag-to-reorder itinerary is pointer-oriented; stops can also be removed with buttons, and a keyboard-friendly reorder control is on our roadmap.
  • The interactive route map (Leaflet/OpenStreetMap) has limited screen-reader support; the full day-by-day text itinerary conveys the same route.
  • Some emoji icons are decorative and may be announced by screen readers; we are improving their labelling.

4. Tips for a better experience

  • Use your browser's zoom (Ctrl/Cmd and +) to enlarge text; the layout reflows cleanly.
  • Every slider shows its current value as text, and most selections can be made with simple button choices.
  • Your progress is saved on your device, so you can take breaks without losing work.
  • Prefer less movement? Enable "reduce motion" in your operating system and the site's animations will switch off.

5. Feedback and contact

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, we would like to know so we can address it. Please tell us via our contact page, describing the page, what you were trying to do, and the assistive technology you use. We aim to respond within a reasonable timeframe.

6. Updates

We review this statement periodically and update the effective date above when we make accessibility improvements.
