Pattaya / Jomtien Travel Guide

Eastern Gulf Coast · Beaches, nightlife and an easy escape from Bangkok

Pattaya has a big reputation — some of it deserved, some of it exaggerated. The city is loud, lively and unapologetically touristy in the centre, while neighbouring Jomtien offers a calmer, more family-friendly beach stretch just a short ride south. Together they make one of the easiest beach destinations from Bangkok. You can be on the sand in under three hours, with plenty of hotels, restaurants and activities already waiting. It’s a practical choice if you want beach time, nightlife options, and day trips without a long journey.

How to Get There

From Bangkok
The simplest options are the air-conditioned buses and minivans that leave regularly from Ekkamai (Eastern Bus Terminal) and Mo Chit. Journey time is usually 2–2.5 hours depending on traffic, and tickets are cheap (around 120–250 baht). Buses also run directly from Suvarnabhumi and Don Mueang airports.

Private transfers and taxis take about 1.5–2.5 hours and cost more, but they’re convenient if you have luggage or a group. Once you arrive at the North Pattaya bus terminal, songthaews (baht buses) are the cheapest and easiest way to reach Beach Road, Walking Street or Jomtien.

Within the area, baht buses run frequent loops between central Pattaya, Jomtien and Naklua for a low fixed fare. Grab is widely available for door-to-door trips.

Climate & Best Time to Visit

Pattaya sits on the Gulf of Thailand and generally has more reliable beach weather than the Andaman coast during the rainy season.

Best time: November to February
Cooler temperatures, lower humidity and the least rain. Perfect for beach days and outdoor activities. This is peak season, so expect higher prices and more crowds.

Hot season: March to May
It gets very hot, often in the mid-to-high 30s. Still usable if you stick to mornings and evenings, and you’ll find better hotel rates.

Rainy season: May/June to October
Afternoon storms are common, especially August and September. Showers are often short and heavy rather than all-day rain. Hotels are cheaper and the beaches quieter. Sea conditions can be choppier at times.

What You’ll Love Doing Here

Jomtien Beach is the better stretch of sand for most visitors who want to actually spend time on the beach. It’s wider, less crowded than central Pattaya Beach, and popular with families and longer-stay visitors. The water is generally calmer and the atmosphere more relaxed.

Central Pattaya is dominated by Beach Road and the famous (or infamous) Walking Street. After dark it turns into a neon-lit strip of bars, clubs and entertainment. It’s loud, colourful and not for everyone — but it’s also one of the most energetic nightlife scenes in Thailand. Even if you only walk through once, it’s an experience.

One of the standout attractions is the Sanctuary of Truth, a massive hand-carved wooden temple overlooking the sea. It’s still being built after decades and the craftsmanship is genuinely impressive. Go early to avoid the heat and the biggest tour groups.

A popular day trip is the short boat ride to Koh Larn (Coral Island). The water is clearer than on the mainland beaches and there are several nice bays for swimming and relaxing. Ferries and speedboats leave regularly from Bali Hai Pier and other points.

Other options include Nong Nooch Tropical Garden, the Big Buddha viewpoint, water parks, and a wide range of restaurants and seafood places. Jomtien has its own quieter evening scene with beachfront restaurants and a night market that feels more local than the central Pattaya options.

Pros and Cons

What people love

  • Very easy and quick to reach from Bangkok
  • Jomtien offers a genuinely more relaxed beach atmosphere
  • Huge range of accommodation, food and activities
  • Good base for a day trip to Koh Larn
  • Something for almost every type of traveller (beach, nightlife, families, longer stays)

What can frustrate people

  • Central Pattaya can feel chaotic, pushy and overly commercial
  • The main Pattaya Beach is not the prettiest in Thailand
  • Walking Street and parts of the nightlife scene are not for everyone
  • Traffic and baht-bus routes can be confusing at first
  • It attracts a mixed crowd and some areas feel quite seedy

Why Go

Pattaya and Jomtien work because they’re practical. You can leave Bangkok after breakfast and be on the beach by lunchtime. Stay in Jomtien if you want a calmer base with a proper beach; use central Pattaya if nightlife and energy are the priority. Add a day on Koh Larn, visit the Sanctuary of Truth, and eat plenty of seafood. It’s not the most refined or natural destination in Thailand, but for many people it’s an easy, entertaining and convenient place to spend a few days by the sea.
