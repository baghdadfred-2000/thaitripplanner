Surat Thani Travel Guide

Southern Thailand · Main mainland gateway to Koh Samui, Koh Phangan and Koh Tao

Surat Thani is the largest city in its province and the main transport hub for the Gulf islands. Most travellers pass through on the way to Koh Samui, Koh Phangan or Koh Tao, using the airport, train station or bus terminal before continuing by ferry from Don Sak or other piers. The city itself is a working Thai provincial centre rather than a tourist resort, with night markets, local temples and a riverside atmosphere. It is practical, authentic and useful — more a place to transit or pause than a primary holiday destination.

How to Get There

Flying in
Surat Thani Airport (URT) has frequent flights from Bangkok (Don Mueang and Suvarnabhumi) with AirAsia, Nok Air, Thai Lion Air and others. Flight time is about 1 hour 15 minutes. Combined bus-and-ferry tickets to the islands are easy to arrange at the airport.

By train
Overnight trains from Bangkok take 8–12 hours depending on the service. Phun Phin station is the main stop, a short songthaew or taxi ride from the city centre.

By bus
Frequent buses leave from Bangkok’s Southern Bus Terminal. Journey time is roughly 9–11 hours.

From the city or airport, most people continue to Don Sak pier (about 1 hour) for ferries to Samui, Phangan and Tao. Shared minibuses and private transfers are widely available.

Climate & Best Time to Visit

Surat Thani follows the Gulf of Thailand pattern.

Best time: December to April
Drier weather and more reliable conditions for onward travel to the islands. January and February are particularly pleasant.

Shoulder and green season: May to September
Still many usable days. The Gulf side is often drier than the Andaman coast during these months.

Wettest period: October to November
Heavier rain from the northeast monsoon. Ferry schedules can occasionally be affected. December improves as the month progresses.

What You’ll Love Doing Here

For most people Surat Thani is a transit point, but it has enough to fill an evening or a quiet day. The night markets are the strongest local attraction — good southern Thai street food, grilled seafood, satay and sweets at everyday prices. The riverside area along the Tapi is pleasant for a walk at sunset.

There are a few temples and the city pillar shrine worth a short visit if you have time. Nearby, Khao Sok National Park (about 1.5–2 hours away) is a major nature destination with jungle, limestone cliffs and a large reservoir, and is often combined with a Surat Thani stop.

Evenings are low-key. Most travellers eat at the markets or a simple restaurant and rest before an early ferry. The city feels genuinely Thai rather than tourist-oriented, which many people appreciate after time on the islands.

Pros and Cons

What people love

  • Convenient and well-connected gateway to the Gulf islands
  • Good value food and night markets
  • Authentic provincial Thai atmosphere
  • Easy combined transport options (flight/train + ferry)
  • Useful base for Khao Sok National Park

What can frustrate people

  • Limited major sightseeing attractions in the city itself
  • Can feel purely functional if you are only passing through
  • Hot and humid, especially outside the cooler months
  • Ferry connections add an extra transfer step
  • Less English and tourist infrastructure than the islands

Why Go

Surat Thani is the practical mainland hub for the popular Gulf islands. Use it as a transit stop when flying or taking the train from Bangkok, grab a good meal at the night market, and continue to Samui, Phangan or Tao the next day. If you have extra time, a side trip to Khao Sok is well worth considering. It is not a destination that demands days of exploration, but it is efficient, authentic and useful — exactly what a good gateway city should be.
