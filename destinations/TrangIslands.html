Trang Islands Travel Guide

Andaman Coast · Quiet islands with clear water, the Emerald Cave and fewer crowds than Phuket or Lanta

The Trang Islands are a small group of Andaman islands off the coast of Trang province, best known for Koh Kradan, Koh Muk (also spelled Koh Mook) and Koh Ngai. They offer white sand, clear water and a more relaxed pace than the better-known islands further north. The standout experience is the Emerald Cave on Koh Muk — a short swim through a dark sea tunnel into a hidden lagoon. The islands are less developed and require a bit more effort to reach, which keeps them quieter for most of the year.

How to Get There

Trang is the mainland gateway. The town has an airport with flights from Bangkok (about 1 hour 25 minutes) and a train station with overnight services from the capital.

From Trang, combined minivan-and-boat tickets take you to the islands. Common departure piers include Pak Meng and Kuan Tung Ku. Journey times from Trang town or the airport to the islands are typically 1.5–3 hours depending on the island and connections.

Seasonal speedboats also link the Trang Islands with Koh Lanta, Phuket and Koh Lipe in high season. Once on the islands there are no cars — everything is walkable or reachable by longtail boat.

Climate & Best Time to Visit

The Trang Islands follow the Andaman coast seasons.

Best time: November to April
Dry weather, calm seas and the full range of boat trips and snorkeling. December to March is peak season.

Shoulder months: November and April
Still good conditions with fewer crowds than the middle of high season.

Rainy season: May to October
Heavier rain and rougher seas. Many boat services reduce or stop, and some resorts close or operate on limited schedules. The islands become very quiet.

What You’ll Love Doing Here

Koh Muk is the most practical base for many visitors. It has a real village, a range of accommodation and restaurants, and is the closest island to the famous Emerald Cave (Tham Morakot). Swimming through the dark tunnel into the hidden lagoon is the highlight of the area — go early to avoid the biggest day-trip crowds.

Koh Kradan is smaller and more exclusive, with exceptionally clear water and excellent snorkeling right off the beach. It feels more intimate but has fewer facilities and higher prices.

Koh Ngai is compact and resort-focused, with long shallow beaches that are good for swimming and families. Other islands such as Koh Libong and Koh Sukorn are quieter still and appeal to travellers looking for a slower, more local experience.

Snorkeling, beach time, simple island walks and boat trips between the islands fill most days. Evenings are low-key — dinner, a drink and an early night are the norm.

Pros and Cons

What people love

  • Beautiful beaches and clear water with fewer crowds than the main Andaman islands
  • The Emerald Cave is a genuinely memorable experience
  • Good snorkeling, especially around Koh Kradan
  • Relaxed, low-key atmosphere
  • More authentic village life on Koh Muk than on purely resort islands

What can frustrate people

  • Reaching the islands involves extra transfers compared with Phuket or Lanta
  • Limited nightlife and facilities, especially on the smaller islands
  • Some beaches are affected by tides or day-tripper boats
  • Many services slow down or close in the rainy season
  • Accommodation on Koh Kradan can be expensive relative to what is offered

Why Go

The Trang Islands are ideal if you want Andaman beaches and clear water without the scale and intensity of Phuket, Phi Phi or even Lanta. Base yourself on Koh Muk for the best mix of practicality and atmosphere, visit the Emerald Cave early, and spend at least one night on Koh Kradan if the budget allows. The extra effort to get here is rewarded with quieter beaches, good snorkeling and a slower pace that many travellers prefer once they experience it.
